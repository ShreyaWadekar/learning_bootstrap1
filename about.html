About Us

Who We Are

We are Butterfly Theory, a science-led collective of naturalists, student researchers, and conservationists dedicated to understanding butterflies and sharing that knowledge with the world. Our work blends theory—evolution, ecology, behavior, and population dynamics—with real‑world conservation and community education.

Vision

To create a world where butterflies thrive as symbols of biodiversity, resilience, and beauty—where communities, schools, and researchers work together to restore ecosystems and ensure that future generations experience the wonder of butterflies in their natural habitats.

Mission

Advance butterfly science and protect their habitats by turning rigorous research into practical action and easy‑to‑understand learning for everyone.

History

From its roots as a student‑led initiative, Butterfly Theory has grown into a recognized platform for butterfly research and conservation. In 2018, a small group of naturalists began documenting local species, sharing field observations and photographs online. By 2020, the group expanded into hosting workshops and collaborating with schools. Today, our history reflects steady growth from community notes to an international knowledge hub, always driven by curiosity and care for biodiversity.
